Kerala

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline. It's known for its palm-lined beaches and backwaters, a network of canals. Inland are the Western Ghats, mountains whose slopes support tea, coffee and spice plantations as well as wildlife. National parks like Eravikulam and Periyar, plus Wayanad and other sanctuaries, are home to elephants, langur monkeys and tigers.

Places to Visit in Kerala

  • Alleppey: Dubbed the 'venice of the East', Alleppey has many backwater channels interconnected in the city where you can rent houseboards
  • Munnar: Known for its tea plantations, Munnar is one of the most visited hillstation.
  • Kumarakom: With alluring flora and fauna as well as numerous lakes, kumarakon is one of the most serene place in kerala

Rajasthan

Rajasthan, known as the "Land of Kings" and India's largest desert state, is a captivating destination in northern India that mesmerise travellers from around the world. The state presents a harmonious blend of lively cities, hospitable locals, exquisite cuisine, majestic palaces, and grand forts. Rajasthan offers a perfect travel experience, seamlessly fusing timeless allure with contemporary charm.

Places to Visit in Rajasthan

  • Jaisalmer: In the heart of thar desert, the city reflects a golden hue from the sunlight and is nicknamed "The Golden City".
  • Jaipur: The Capital of Rajasthan is home to many places.
  • Jodhpur: Called the 'blue city' thanks to its buildings that aere painted with the colour, Jodhpur is an absolute delight to visit.

Goa

Goa is a state in western India with coastlines stretching along the Arabian Sea. Its long history as a Portuguese colony prior to 1961 is evident in its preserved 17th-century churches and the area’s tropical spice plantations. Goa is also known for its beaches, ranging from popular stretches at Baga and Palolem to those in laid-back fishing villages such as Agonda.

Places to Visit in Goa

  • Palolem Beach: Excellent for a quieter getaway. The wide, flat stretches of sand are perfect for building sandcastles.
  • Dudhsagar Falls: Take an exciting, bumpy Jeep safari through the lush forests.
  • Morjim & Ashvem Beaches (North Goa):Called the 'blue city' thanks to its buildings that aere painted with the colour, Jodhpur is an absolute delight to visit.

Punjab

Punjab, a state bordering Pakistan, is the heart of India’s Sikh community. The city of Amritsar, founded in the 1570s by Sikh Guru Ram Das, is the site of Harmandir Sahib, the holiest gurdwara (Sikh place of worship). Known in English as the Golden Temple, and surrounded by the Pool of Nectar, it's a major pilgrimage site. Also in Amritsar is Durgiana Temple, a Hindu shrine famed for its engraved silver doors.

Places to Visit in Punjab

  • Amritsar: Home to famous landmarks like the golden temple and the jallianwala Bagh
  • Chandigarh: Considered the most visited city in indi, chandigarh is also home to the famous Rock Gardens and the Leisure valley.
  • Jalandhar: One of the most ancient cities in india - full of history and culture.
